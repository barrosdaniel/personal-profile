Personal Information

Hi, I'm Daniel Barros. I’m a Front-end Web Developer based out of Perth, Australia. I bring to life fun, innovative, accessible, and fast websites. I try to leave every bit of code I touch more readable, modular, performant, accessible and maintainable than I found it.

When I'm not bringing design ideas into life, I like to read about technology or watch science documentaries or sports.

After approximately 20 years working in Human Resources, I have switched careers into IT working with Information Systems. I’m now pursuing Bachelor Degree in Information Technology, attempting to concentrate further on Computer Science subjects. My career goal is to move into a more technical role, initially as a Software Developer and then move onto corporate Software Architecture.

I was born and grew up in tropical Brazil where I finished school, my first Bachelor Degree in Business (Human Resources) and a Master Degree in Marketing, before coming to Australia and doing a second Master Degree in Human Resources. Apart from English, I also speak Portuguese (native) and can speak some Spanish, which has many similarities to Portuguese.

Outside of my career, when I was younger, I played Futsal (an indoor variation of soccer) at a competitive level, having won the WA State League 2 times and competing in a national league, both as a player and coach.

Interest in IT

My interest in IT started when I was a kid playing games and fiddling around with my parents’ personal computer. I just found it amazing back in the day that so much information could be put in a digital format: images, video, music and so much more. I soon learned that it had many Productivity uses as well, and connectivity. With the internet, everything changed. Suddenly everything got connected, everyone got connected. Businesses got connected. I also found incredible the speed in which progress was made.

Since then I developed a keen interest in everything tech-related. This should have been sufficient for me to pick IT as a career. However, I ended up pursuing other interests at the time.

A couple of years ago I started taking online courses on Web Development and I really enjoyed it. Now, I am finally prioritising a career that (I believe) I am more suited to. I am enrolled in the Bachelor of IT with RMIT and have also recently changed careers into IT, working with healthcare Information Systems. The systems area interest me a lot as I worked with enterprise systems and data analysis throughout my whole career. Outside of work, I am interested in games and interface design. Although I’ve had limited contact with, I find the practical applications of Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence exciting.

I chose RMIT online through Open Universities Australia (OUA) as I believe this is the best option currently in the market to study IT and Computer Science online at a Bachelor Degree level. I find that having 4 study periods throughout the year can speed up my studies as I am only available part-time. Both RMIT and OUA have extensive experience in the online delivery of the course. In addition, the RMIT is nationally and internationally recognised for its excellence in academic and research in Technology.

During my studies, I plan to learn a lot about Technology. I will endeavour to focus my studies around topics including Programming and Software Design and Development as much as possible and to be exposed to technology stacks used by industry, such as Java, the Microsoft stack, Mobile applications, and open source web technologies. I also have a keen interest in Intelligent Systems and Artificial Intelligence, and Internet of Things with simple Robotics. Unfortunately, currently these are not topics available through RMIT/OUA Bachelor of IT course. But, given the rise of importance of these topics, it is possible that before I graduate, units covering these topics may be introduced as electives.
